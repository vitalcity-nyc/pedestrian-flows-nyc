When New York walks

Saturday evening, not rush hour, is the city's peak walking hour.

Estimated pedestrians on NYC sidewalks, summed citywide (millions of segment-hours)

At an estimated 73.8 million pedestrian-segments between 5 and 6 p.m. Saturday, weekend evenings carry roughly 27% more foot traffic than the weekday evening commute. Weekend mornings are the quietest stretch the model captures.

03 / 06

Why New Yorkers walk

Commuting home leads, but trips to parks and amenities nearly tie.

Estimated share of pedestrian volume by journey type (millions of trip-segments)

Home-to-work trips are the single largest category — but home-to-parks and home-to-amenities (shops, restaurants, services) together outweigh every work-related trip type combined.

04 / 06

The weekend flip

Four boroughs walk more on weekends. Manhattan walks less.

Change in estimated evening pedestrian volume, weekday 5-6 p.m. → weekend 5-6 p.m.

The outer boroughs' sidewalks fill up on Saturday nights — Staten Island's evening foot traffic nearly doubles. Only Manhattan sees pedestrians thin out, as the office-district and transit-hub crowds that define its weekday rush disappear.

About this model. MIT City Form Lab built the network from NYC's LION planimetric sidewalk layer, added missing park paths, and validated every segment against 2019 aerial imagery. Pedestrian volumes for six weekday and weekend time slots were estimated using a calibrated spatial model of home-to-destination trips. Journey-type breakdowns (home-to-job, home-to-parks, etc.) use uncalibrated O-D estimates; absolute values can differ from observed volumes, but relative spatial patterns are meaningful.

Source: Sevtsuk, Basu, Liu & Kollar (2025), "Spatial Distribution of Foot-traffic in New York City and Applications for Urban Planning," Nature Cities.
