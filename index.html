Pedestrian flows / New York City

18 walking stories, one map of New York.

A block-by-block model from MIT's City Form Lab estimates hourly pedestrian volume on every one of New York's 315,577 sidewalk and crosswalk segments. We mined it for 18 blocks that each tell a different story — the citywide busiest, the morning-rush kings, the weekend-only destinations, the office blocks that go dead on Saturday, and the busiest foot traffic in every outer borough. Click any numbered pin for the street, the volumes, and the angle.

Source: Sevtsuk, A., Basu, R., et al. Estimating Pedestrian Flows on Street Networks in New York City (Nature Cities, 2025), based on the NYC Pedestrian Network Estimates dataset (2018–2019). Volumes are modeled estimates calibrated against 1,011 field counts, not direct observation. Streets are named via OpenStreetMap reverse-geocoding of each segment's midpoint. Because the model leans on network accessibility and transit proximity, it can under-predict recreational and seasonal foot traffic (beaches, parks, boardwalks) — a limitation the authors note — so outer-borough leisure peaks are likely larger in reality than the figures shown here.  ·  Produced for Vital City.
