Pedestrian flows / New York City

How New York walks

A block-by-block model of foot traffic across 315,577 sidewalk segments reveals a city whose busiest hours are not the ones you'd expect.

The peak hour isn't rush hour

Estimated pedestrians on NYC sidewalks, summed across the five boroughs (millions of segment-hours)

Saturday evening is New York's busiest walking hour — an estimated 73.8 million pedestrian-segments between 5 and 6 p.m., roughly 27% more than the weekday evening commute. Weekend mornings are the quietest stretch the model captures.

Where New Yorkers walk to

Share of estimated pedestrian volume by journey type. Commuting home from work narrowly leads — but trips to parks and neighborhood amenities nearly match it.

Home-to-job trips account for roughly 20% of all modeled foot traffic. Trips to parks (19%) and to restaurants, shops and services (19%) together outweigh every commute category combined.

The busiest sidewalks in the city are all within 10 blocks

Location of the 50 highest-volume sidewalk segments at weekday midday. Every one sits inside a half-square-mile of Midtown Manhattan.

Top 50 segments (~2,400+ pedestrians/hr) Manhattan outline

The busiest modeled block in the city runs along 7th Avenue near West 49th Street, with an estimated 2,800 pedestrians passing every midday hour. Of 315,577 segments citywide, only 12 exceed 2,500 pedestrians per hour.
